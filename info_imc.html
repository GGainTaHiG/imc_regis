What is CMU-IMC?



CMU-IMC stands for "Chiang Mai University International Medical Challenge", which is an annual international academic competition held by the Faculty of Medicine, Chiang Mai University. Competitors are medical students from various countries around the world. The first CMU-IMC was held as part of the 55th Anniversary of the establishment of the Faculty of Medicine, Chiang Mai University. Theme used in each year differs, starting from
CMU-IMC 2014: Pathophysiology and Pharmacology
CMU-IMC 2015: Basic to Clinical Biochemistry
CMU-IMC 2016: Basic to Clinical Anatomy
CMU-IMC 2017: Basic to Clinical Hematology and Transfusion Medicine
CMU-IMC 2018: Basic to Clinical Infectious Disease
CMU-IMC 2019: Basic to Clinical Physiology
Sadly, due to COVID-19 pandemic, CMU-IMC 2020 and 2021 were cancelled and the 2022 CMU-IMC will be held via online platform in April 2022 in the theme Basic to Clinical Biochemistry and Molecular Biology
By Joining the event, you will not only earn medical knowledge but also friendship and experiences from other participants, and remember, it is victory unclamied for someone who dares to try!

Who organizes this event?

CMU-IMC is organized by Faculty of Medicine, Chiang Mai University.


The Faculty of Medicine, Chiang Mai University is the third oldest medical school in Thailand and is the first medical school established in a regional location outside of Bangkok located in Muang Chiang Mai, Chiang Mai. It was established in 1959 to serve the need for more physicians to serve the rural areas, especialy in the northern region of Thailand. The Faculty has grown continuously and has fully achieved its original mandate and is now the largest medical school in northern Thailand, operating the largest and most comprehensive hospital in this region.
